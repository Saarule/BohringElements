Introduction

The Bohring Elements are on-chain generated NFTs

The project was inspired by Niels Henrik David Bohr which lived between 7 October 1885 to 18 November 1962. Bohr was a physicist who made foundational contributions to understanding atomic structure and quantum theory, for which he received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1922. Bohr was also a philosopher and a promoter of scientific research.

Bohr developed the Bohr model of the atom, in which he proposed that energy levels of electrons are discrete and that the electrons revolve in stable orbits around the atomic nucleus but can jump from one energy level (or orbit) to another.

Mint Elements

The Bohring Elements in a nutshell

The Bohring Elements are “on-chain” generative artworks on the Polygon blockchain.

Created by a completely self-contained mechanism for creating and owning an artwork.

The Bohring Elements are an experiment in generative art; each is unique and created by code running on the Polygon network.

As more time passes and more Bohring Elements are minted, the more expensive it becomes to mint new Bohring Elements.

The first 1000 elements are free to mint, and after that, for every 1000, the minting price increases by 10% due to code instructions.
